Overview

Technology in the classroom divides more than it connects.

With more than 43% of Gen Alpha owning some form of technology before kindergarten, children are becoming increasingly accustomed to using technology in their daily lives—including their education. However, with the rise of Chromebooks and iPads in classrooms, which lack effective ways to foster a cooperative learning environment, a barrier has emerged between students and teachers.

The question then becomes: How do we help students stay focused using technology while also making it flexible and intuitive for teachers to use?

Research

I interviewed six tutors and teachers who taught children aged 5-15. Four primarily taught students online (via Google Meet, Zoom, etc.), while two taught in person.

Children work better when they're engaged.

Both online and in-person teachers found that students performed better when lessons were gamified—using tools like Kahoot or collaborative learning activities.

Tried and true > expensive and new.

Both groups of teachers emphasized the importance of structured lesson plans to maintain a consistent learning environment, especially in larger classes. Even if less exciting, reusable templates are cost-effective and easier to implement across classrooms.

Online learning creates barriers that can't be fully solved.

A major pain point for online teachers was the lack of control. Since they can't physically monitor students' device use, much of their classroom management depends on student behavior.

Solution

Meet Octobud, a code-and-scanner learning device that gives students and teachers a gamified and flexible learning system for any situation.

Process

Realizing the design

When developing the optimal design for Octobud, careful consideration was given to balancing the input and output systems. The goal was to make them simple and intuitive enough for children to understand while maintaining functionality for teachers.

Next Steps

Connecting to existing platforms

Since not all classrooms would immediately transition to this system, adding synchronization features with existing tools would improve adoption. By allowing Octobud to integrate with established educational platforms, teachers could streamline lesson management and reduce errors caused by switching between tools.

Takeaways

Don't reinvent the wheel

My early designs focused too heavily on aesthetics instead of usability. By shifting focus to the user journey and incorporating student feedback throughout the process, the final design evolved into a product tailored to real classroom needs.
