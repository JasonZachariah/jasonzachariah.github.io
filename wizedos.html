RESEARCH

The Scope

This project was assigned to me during my time at WizRobotics. They wanted to revamp a website for their service, WizEdOS, an all-in-one learning platform for STEM educators to create, host, and track content for STEM learning.

Initially, when discussing the needs of the project, it was thought that a simple redesign was. However, after analyzing the website and discussing long-term goals for the website, there was a large disconnect between the current structure and the goals that needed to be fulfilled

Problems

  1. Lack of Clarity

    2. Many of the images used were stock illustrations that were inconsistent and did not help show the services provided.

  2. Confusing Page Setup

    3. The ordering of the page content was inconsistent, and much vital information was placed in a hard-to-reach area. For example, the pricing section was placed in a separate tab, making it difficult for users to navigate. .

Solution

WizEdOS, the all-in-one platform for STEM learning

process

Treat it Like a Story

Based on the pain points of the current systems in place, I drew the current user flow based on the student testimonials I gathered to understand where the issues stem from.

FLOWS

Wireframes

Before designing, I sat down with the client and decided on the diffrent content of the website. In this way, the client and I could rearrange the sections in different ways and understand what key points, and have a better way of understnadning the flow of the webdsite

From this, we came to two major flows for the WizEdOS website. First, to get companies to book a meeting. And secondly, to show of the four subservices of WizEdOs

For the first flow, this was achived by adding a CTA button in the hero section as well as the nav bar of the website, which would open a pop-up modal, allowing for users to easily acess the flow without having to search for it.

Takeaways

Design should be a team effort

What made the design process move smoothly was the transparency of the design process, having the client have a direct look into why I made certain design decisions. This allowed for fewer changes later on in the process, and caused both of us to be clear on what was being delivered.
