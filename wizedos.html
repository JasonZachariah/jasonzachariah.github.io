Overview

WizEDOS, a b2b serivce for STEM education, lacked a consistent design system that alieneted new businesses from their platform

During my time at WizRobotics,I had the chance to create a new direction for WizEdOS, an all-in-one learning platform for STEM educators. Initially, when discussing the scope of the project, the client just thought a simple visual refresh was needed, with some minor edits to the website. However, after analyzing the website and discussing long-term goals for the service, there was a large disconnect with what changes were nessary for the service.

inital notes

So,what were the issues with the old WizEdOS system.

After reviewing the intial website together with the client,here were the key pain points that were mentioned:

  1. Inconsistent Branding

    2. Many of the images used in the website random stock illustrations that did not communicate the services provided. As a result, the website did not look cohesive, giving the website a "cheap" image.

  2. Confusing Page Setup

    3. Many important sections of the website, such as the booking form, were placed in hard to acess regions, making it harder for users to locate key actions quickly.

Since we now had a common understanding of what elements of the website needed changing, I could now start the research and devlopment phase of this project.

Creating the Flows

Treat the website layout like a converstation

With this method, I was able to naturally find ways to group content, making notes on the major points, and grouping them together based on the way he broke down he explanation.

In this way, we naturally found ways to group content together, allowing for a more structure in the website. These note nataed allowed for more ease of access when moving on to to the wireframing, creating less revison work later on in the process.

FLOWS

Wireframes

From this, we came to two major flows for the WizEdOS website. First, to get companies to book a meeting. And secondly, to show of the four subservices of WizEdOs

For the first flow, this was achived by adding a CTA button in the hero section as well as the nav bar of the website, which would open a pop-up modal, allowing for users to easily acess the flow without having to search for it.

Takeaways

Work with your client, not against them

What made the design process move smoothly was the transparency of the design process, having the client have a direct look into why I made certain design decisions. This allowed for fewer changes later on in the process, and caused both of us to be clear on what was being delivered.
